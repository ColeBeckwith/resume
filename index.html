Summary

I graduated from The University of Texas at Austin a year early at 20. I felt way ahead of the pack and confident that the momentum would continue. I moved to Los Angeles to pursue a career in screenwriting, and things came to a grinding halt. I realized that as much as I loved the craft, I didn't love the industry surrounding it. I was scrambling to find a new career.

Someone suggested computer programming. I took a few online courses and thought, 'Wow, this is actually pretty easy!' Of course, it wasn't. The "Hello, World!" type programs I was writing had even less value than the two dozen screenplays in the trunk of my car. When I discovered Angular and responsive design, they made my head spin, but piece by piece I began to understand them.

One of the most important skills a developer can have is the ability to learn quickly and independently. That's easy enough to put on a resume or spew off in an interview, but to lend some credence to that claim, I leave this site almost exactly as I had written it 19 days after I couldn't tell HTML from CSS from JavaScript.
