Summary

I graduated from The University of Texas at Austin with a degree in Radio-Television-Film where I focused on screenwriting. I spent several months working in Los Angeles, before deciding that it wasn't for me.

That realization would have been more helpful before I'd spent four years working towards a degree that has no perceived value outside of Hollywood. I had taken a lot of business classes, but I was competing against applicants with much more specific backgrounds for the marketing, finance and accounting positions.

Someone suggested programming. I figured I was too far behind to become a competitive candidate, but I looked into it. I was surprised to find how much of it I already knew. A couple of C++ classes in high school, a part-time job managing a website, an MIS class that focused on MySQL, and several digital media classes. I filled in the gaps with a lot of Googling and eventually felt comfortable doing it professionally.
